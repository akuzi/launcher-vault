# OEM Launchers

The **default launchers shipped by phone makers**. Most users never change them, so they define the home experience for the majority of Android devices. Important context for why people seek third-party launchers.

## Major examples
- **One UI Home** (Samsung) — polished, feature-rich, app drawer optional, Good Lock/“Home Up” module adds deep customization. Generally well-regarded.
- **HyperOS / MIUI Home** (Xiaomi) — historically iOS-like (no drawer by default), feature-heavy; criticised for **ads and recommendation feeds** in some regions ([[Privacy and Launchers]], [[Monetization Models|ad-supported]]).
- **OxygenOS / ColorOS** (OnePlus / Oppo) — clean-ish, shelf/feed panel.
- **Funtouch / Realme UI**, **MagicOS** (Honor), etc.

## Why they matter to research
- **Baseline** users compare against — third-party launchers win by being cleaner, more customizable, less ad-laden, or more minimal.
- **Strategic / vendor-funded** ([[Monetization Models]]): part of the device's differentiation; sometimes monetised via ads/feeds, which conflicts with [[Digital Wellbeing and Minimalism]].
- **Integration advantage**: OEM launchers hook into device-specific features (Samsung DeX, edge panels, themes) that third parties can't always match.

## Why people leave them
- Ads/bloat/feeds (notably some MIUI builds).
- Limited customization (or limited *without* the OEM's own theme store).
- Wanting a consistent experience across different-brand devices.

# Niagara Launcher

A **minimalist, one-handed** launcher built around a short vertical list and an alphabetical scrub. Distraction-reducing but still highly usable as a daily driver.

## At a glance
- **Category**: [[Launcher Taxonomy|Minimalist / intentional]]
- **Retrieval model**: list + alphabetical scrub (see [[Launcher Mental Models]])
- **Developer**: Peter Huber / Niagara Launcher team

## Pricing
- **Free** core experience.
- **Niagara Pro** — subscription (roughly ~$10/yr) with a **lifetime** option (~$30, prices vary by region/time). See [[Pricing Comparison]].
- Model: [[Monetization Models|subscription]].

## Key features
- **Vertical list** of favourite/frequent apps on the home screen, no grid clutter.
- **Alphabetical scrub bar** on the right edge — drag to jump to any app one-handed.
- **Pop-out app menu** with shortcuts and contacts.
- **One-handed** design optimised for large phones (reachable thumb zone).
- Clean integration of notifications inline with apps.
- Media controls, At-a-Glance-style info.

## Mental model fit
Sits between minimalism and usability. Attacks the [[Digital Wellbeing and Minimalism|cue / muscle-memory]] problem (no fixed icon grid) while keeping a fast retrieval path (the scrub). A more *practical* minimalism than text-only [[Olauncher]].

## Best for
"I want fewer distractions and one-handed use, without giving up speed." Compare with [[Olauncher]] (stricter, text-only) and [[Before Launcher]].

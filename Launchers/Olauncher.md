# Olauncher

A **radically minimal, text-only** launcher. No icons, no app drawer grid — just a few words on a blank screen. Built for using your phone *less*.

## At a glance
- **Category**: [[Launcher Taxonomy|Minimalist / intentional]]
- **Retrieval model**: short text list + search (see [[Launcher Mental Models]])
- **Developer**: open-source community (Tanujnotes and contributors)

## Pricing
- **Free**, open source. Model: [[Monetization Models|FOSS]] (donation-supported).

## Key features
- **Home screen** is a list of up to ~8 chosen apps as plain text.
- **No icons, no colors** — removes visual cues (see [[Digital Wellbeing and Minimalism]]).
- Swipe-based app search for everything else.
- Gestures for a couple of quick actions; minimal settings.
- Tiny, fast, low permissions ([[Privacy and Launchers]]).

## Mental model fit
The strict end of the [[Digital Wellbeing and Minimalism]] spectrum and axis 2 ([[Launcher Mental Models|intentional friction]]). By stripping color and position, it kills the unconscious "open the app" reflex. Often paired with system **grayscale**.

## Trade-offs
- Deliberately *slower* to reach non-favourite apps — that friction is the point.
- No theming/icon packs (by design).

## Best for
"I want my phone to be boring." Compare with [[Niagara Launcher]] (minimal but fast/usable) and [[Before Launcher]].

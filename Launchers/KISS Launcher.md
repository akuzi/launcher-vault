# KISS Launcher

"Keep It Simple, Stupid." A **search-first**, ultra-lightweight FOSS launcher: type a few letters and the thing you want surfaces instantly.

## At a glance
- **Category**: [[Launcher Taxonomy|Search-first]]
- **Retrieval model**: search (see [[Launcher Mental Models]])
- **Developer**: open-source community

## Pricing
- **Free**, open source. Model: [[Monetization Models|FOSS]].

## Key features
- **Universal search bar** — finds apps, contacts, settings, shortcuts; can trigger calls, web search.
- **Adaptive results** — learns and ranks by your usage.
- Extremely small APK, **works offline**, no tracking ([[Privacy and Launchers]]).
- Minimal home screen; favourites bar.

## Mental model fit
The purest example of the **search-first retrieval model** (axis 1, [[Launcher Mental Models]]): position is irrelevant, scales to any number of apps, but adds a typing step. Also minimalist in spirit (see [[Digital Wellbeing and Minimalism]]).

## Trade-offs
- Search-centric flow doesn't suit users who prefer [[Launcher Mental Models|spatial]] muscle memory.
- Sparse visuals; no heavy theming.

## Best for
"I'd rather type three letters than hunt for an icon." Compare with [[AIO Launcher]] (also text-led but info-dense) and [[Olauncher]] (minimal but list-based).
